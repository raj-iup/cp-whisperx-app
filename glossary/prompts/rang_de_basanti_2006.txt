Film: Rang De Basanti (2006)
Director: Rakeysh Omprakash Mehra
Stars: Aamir Khan, Siddharth, Kunal Kapoor, Sharman Joshi, Atul Kulkarni, Soha Ali Khan, Alice Patten
Setting: Delhi, Delhi University, contemporary with freedom fighter flashbacks
Tone: Youthful energy shifts to revolutionary anger, political awakening
Language: Delhi youth Hinglish, political rhetoric, historical freedom fighter dialogue

Characters:
- DJ/Daljit (male, Aamir): Carefree to revolutionary transformation
- Karan (male, Siddharth): Rich, apolitical to awakened
- Aslam (male, Kunal): Idealistic, patriotic
- Sukhi (male, Sharman): Comic, pilot, martyred
- Laxman Pandey (male, Atul): Dalit, political anger
- Sue (female, Alice): British filmmaker, catalyst
- Sonia (female, Soha): Love interest

Translation Notes:
- Two-tone film: comedy first half, intense second half
- Early: casual youth Hinglish, carefree
- Post-awakening: political fire, revolutionary Hindi
- Historical scenes: period-appropriate formal Hindi/Urdu
- "Rang De Basanti" title: "Paint it saffron/Color me revolutionary"
- Sue's broken Hindi: keep endearing, not perfect
- Laxman's anger: raw, class-conscious
- Aslam's idealism: pure, patriotic without jingoism
- DJ's transformation: show in language shift (casual to serious)
- Radio station scene: urgent, revolutionary broadcast
- Keep Bhagat Singh's actual quotes if used
- "Maa Tujhe Salaam" patriotic tone but not propaganda
- Final act: tragic, not melodramatic
- Balance youth irreverence with political seriousness
