Film: Dil Chahta Hai (2001)
Year: 2001
Setting: Mumbai/Goa/Sydney - urban youth, diaspora
Tone: Modern, sophisticated, natural code-switching, realistic friendships

Main Characters:
- Akash Malhotra (male): Carefree, commitment-phobic, witty
- Sameer Mulchandani (male): Romantic, emotional, artistic
- Siddharth "Sid" Sinha (male): Mature, architect, emotionally reserved

Female Leads:
- Shalini (female): Alcoholic, artistic, Sid's love interest
- Pooja (female): Engaged, Akash's love interest
- Priya (female): Sameer's girlfriend

Key Terms & Usage:
- "yaar" → "dude" or "man" (very frequent, casual male friendship)
- "bhai" → "bro" (among close friends)
- "scene" → keep as "scene" or "situation"
- "fundoo" → "cool" or "smart"
- "mast" → "awesome" or "cool"
- "timepass" → "killing time" or "hanging out"

Speech Patterns:
- Urban Mumbai dialect
- Frequent English phrases mixed with Hindi
- Sophisticated vocabulary (art, architecture references)
- Banter and teasing among friends
- Emotional moments in Hindi
- Goa scenes: relaxed, beachy slang

Cultural Context:
- Upper middle class lifestyle
- Art gallery visits
- Opera house scenes  
- International travel (Sydney)
- Modern dating attitudes
- Male friendship bonds

Location-Specific:
- Mumbai: Urban, fast-paced
- Goa: Relaxed, vacation vibe
- Sydney: Diaspora, professional

Translation Guidelines:
1. Maintain sophisticated tone (not overly casual)
2. "yaar" best as "man" (mature friendship)
3. Keep technical terms (architecture, art) precise
4. Emotional Hindi moments: translate sentiment, not literal
5. Opera scenes: formal register
6. Friend banter: casual but intelligent
7. Sydney scenes: code-switch less, more English

Sample Dialogue:
- "Yaar, tu samajhta kyon nahin?" → "Man, why don't you understand?"
- "Kya fundoo idea hai" → "That's a cool idea"
- "Bas kar yaar, drama mat kar" → "Stop it, man, don't be dramatic"
- "Arre, chal na" → "Hey, let's go" or "Come on"

Catchphrases:
- "Dil chahta hai" (title phrase - keep as is or "The heart wants")
- References to friendship
- Art and architecture terminology
