Film: Dangal (2016)
Director: Nitesh Tiwari
Stars: Aamir Khan, Fatima Sana Shaikh, Sanya Malhotra, Zaira Wasim, Sakshi Tanwar
Setting: Haryana (rural), wrestling akharas, national/international competitions
Tone: Inspirational sports drama, feminist themes, father-daughter relationship
Language: Haryanvi Hindi dialect, wrestling terminology, patriarchal society language shifts

Characters:
- Mahavir Singh Phogat (male, Aamir): Former wrestler, strict father, coach
- Geeta Phogat (female, Fatima/Zaira): Elder daughter, wrestler, rebellious phase
- Babita Phogat (female, Sanya): Younger daughter, wrestler, more compliant
- Daya Kaur (female, Sakshi): Wife, supportive mother

Key Terms - Haryanvi Dialect:
- "bhai" → "brother"
- "ji" → "sir/yes" (respectful)
- "bapu" → "father" (Haryanvi)
- "mhari" → "my/our" (Haryanvi possessive)
- "chori/chora" → "girl/boy" (Haryanvi)
- "tau" → "uncle" (father's elder brother, Haryanvi)

Wrestling Terms:
- "akhara" → wrestling arena/gym (keep or translate)
- "pehelwan" → wrestler
- "dangal" → wrestling bout/competition
- "kushti" → wrestling (traditional Indian)
- "dhakka" → push/shove (technique)
