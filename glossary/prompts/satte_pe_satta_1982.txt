Film: Satte Pe Satta (1982)
Director: Raj N. Sippy
Stars: Amitabh Bachchan, Hema Malini
Setting: Rural farmland, then urban Mumbai
Tone: Comedy, family drama, rustic to refined transformation
Language: Rural Hindi/Haryanvi dialect transitioning to urban Hindi-English

Characters:
- Ravi (male, main): Eldest of 7 brothers, rustic then refined
- Indu (female, main): Educated nurse, city-bred
- Six younger brothers: Rough, uneducated, comedic
- Babu (male): Sophisticated lookalike villain

Key Terms:
- "bhai" → "brother" (always, strong fraternal bonds)
- "bhaiya" → "big brother" (younger siblings to Ravi)
- "didi" → "sister" (brothers to Indu after marriage)
- "ji" → "ma'am/sir" (rural respect, then urban)
- "sahab" → "sir" (working class)
- "mem sahab" → "madam" (colonial-era respect for Indu)

Cultural Context:
- Seven brothers living together (joint family system)
- Transformation theme: rustic to refined under wife's influence
- Class comedy: rural manners vs city etiquette
- Mistaken identity plot (lookalike twin)

Dialect Notes:
- Early scenes: rural/rustic Hindi (less English, more earthiness)
- Post-marriage: gradual refinement but retain brotherly banter
- Indu's dialogue: educated, modern, light Hinglish
- Villain's dialogue: sophisticated, urban

Translation Notes:
- Preserve comedic timing in rustic dialogue
- Show transformation in language (rough → polished)
- Keep brotherhood warmth ("bhai" is sacred)
- Distinguish between twins through speech patterns (rustic vs refined)
- Family dynamics: respect for Indu as "bhabi" (sister-in-law) then "didi"
