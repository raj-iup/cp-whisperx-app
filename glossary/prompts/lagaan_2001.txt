Film: Lagaan (2001)
Director: Ashutosh Gowariker
Stars: Aamir Khan, Gracy Singh, Rachel Shelley
Setting: 1893 British Raj India, rural village Champaner
Tone: Period drama, sports underdog story, anti-colonial resistance
Language: Period Hindi/Urdu, formal British English, minimal modern slang

Characters:
- Bhuvan (male, Aamir): Village farmer, cricket team leader, patriotic
- Elizabeth (female, Rachel): British officer's sister, sympathetic to Indians
- Captain Andrew Russell (male): Arrogant British officer, villain
- Village elders and farmers: Rural, traditional speech

Key Terms - Hindi/Urdu (Period):
- "huzoor" → "sir" (addressing British officers)
- "sahab" → "sir" (respectful to authority)
- "ji" → "yes sir/ma'am" (always)
- "malik" → "master/lord"
- "gora sahab" → "white master" (British officers)
- "firangi" → "foreigner" (British)

British Raj Terms:
- "lagaan" → "tax/land revenue" (keep or translate as "tax")
- "raja" → "king"
- "rani" → "queen"
- "cantonment" → military area (British term, keep)

Cultural/Historical Context:
- 1890s rural India under British rule
- Agricultural economy, drought, oppressive taxation
- Cricket as colonial sport Indians must learn
- Village self-governance vs British authority
- Hindu-Muslim unity (pre-partition harmony)
- Untouchable caste inclusion (Kachra's arc)

Language Register:
- British characters: Formal Victorian English, colonial arrogance
- Elizabeth: Sympathetic, learning Hindi, broken but sweet
- Bhuvan: Earthy Hindi but dignified, not subservient
- Village elders: Formal, traditional Hindi/Urdu
- British soldiers: Commands, military register
- Farmers: Simple, rustic Hindi

Translation Notes:
- Period-appropriate language (1890s feel)
- British dialogue: Maintain colonial superiority tone initially
- Elizabeth's broken Hindi: Keep endearing, show learning curve
- "Gaan" = village, "Lagaan" = tax (wordplay in title)
- Cricket terminology: Keep English (the sport is the point)
- "Ghanan Ghanan" song: monsoon celebration, translate poetically
- Unity theme: Hindu-Muslim-Sikh farmers together (show in names/terms)
- Kachra (untouchable): show dignity despite caste oppression
- British defeat: comeuppance without excessive gloating
- Patriotism without jingoism - resistance is dignified, not hateful
- Keep Victorian-era British speech formal ("capital", "jolly good")
- Village Hindi: earthy, simple, but not "dumb" - show intelligence
- Cricket match: sports commentary style, excitement
