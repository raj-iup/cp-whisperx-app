Film: Dilwale Dulhania Le Jayenge (1995)
Director: Aditya Chopra
Stars: Shah Rukh Khan, Kajol
Setting: London, Punjab (India), trains across Europe
Tone: Romantic, family values, NRI culture, traditional vs modern
Language: Urban NRI English-Hindi mix, Punjabi influences, traditional family Hindi

Characters:
- Raj Malhotra (male, SRK): London-raised, spoiled, charming, transforms
- Simran Singh (female, Kajol): Traditional but modern, dutiful daughter
- Baldev Singh (male): Simran's strict traditional father
- Chaudhry Baldev Singh family: Conservative Punjabi values

Key Terms:
- "ji" → "yes/sir/ma'am" (ALWAYS respectful, very frequent)
- "beta" → "son/daughter" (elders to younger)
- "bhai" → "brother"
- "babuji" → "father" (respectful term)
- "bauji" → "father" (Punjabi variant)
- "sardarji" → "Mr. Singh" (respectful to Sikh men)

NRI Context:
- "Ja Simran ja, jee le apni zindagi" → famous dialogue, keep powerful
- London English: more frequent, casual
- India scenes: shift to more Hindi, family register
- Generational gap in language use

Cultural Context:
- NRI (Non-Resident Indian) identity crisis
- Arranged marriage vs love marriage
- Respecting father's wishes vs following heart
- Punjab culture: honor, family, tradition
- Hindu-Sikh family dynamics (mostly harmonious)

Translation Notes:
- "Palat" (turn around) moment: ICONIC - handle with weight
- Father-daughter relationship: deep respect in language
- Raj's transformation: playboy English to respectful Hindi
- Simran's conflict: dutiful daughter Hindi vs free spirit English
- "Tujhe Dekha To" song: translate romantically, not literally
- Punjab scenes: more traditional, formal Hindi/Punjabi
- London scenes: casual Hinglish, youth culture
- Train journey: transition zone (languages mixing)
- Mandate scene: father's authority in formal Hindi
- Final approval: "Ja Simran ja" - liberation in Hindi, meaningful
- Keep honorifics abundant - respect is the film's core
- Family values: show through formal address patterns
